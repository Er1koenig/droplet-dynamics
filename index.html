Droplet Dynamics

Simulation of sub‑Kolmogorov deformable droplets & bubbles
coupled with an in‑house Lattice Boltzmann solver

Project Overview

This project extends the m‑AIA multi‑physics framework with a two‑way coupled Euler‑Lagrangian module that tracks deformable, Kolmogorov‑sized bubbles and droplets. Key elements include:

• Volume‑conserving shape evolution based on the Maffettone–Minale model
• Novel correction term that eliminates rotation‑induced geometry error during pure vortex motion:contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
• Stable motion equation reformulated for high-density‑ratio regimes:contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
• Validation in quiescent, shear and vortex flows, rising‑bubble benchmark, and DNS of decaying isotropic turbulence:contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Rotation‑error Correction

Eliminates spurious aspect‑ratio drift during pure rotation, ensuring physically consistent shape dynamics.

Oblate Rising Bubble

Captures path‑instability and drag‑increase as the bubble evolves toward an oblate shape in laminar rise tests.

Droplets in Isotropic Turbulence

DNS shows smaller droplets accelerate TKE decay, while large droplets retain kinetic energy through inertia.

Constant‑Load Cases

Suite of canonical flows (shear, vortex, quiescent) used for systematic verification and parameter sweeps.

Main Findings

  • Shape‑controlled drag & lift models predict terminal velocity trends across Ca ≤ 0.1.
  • Volume‑control scheme cuts numerical mass loss by up to two orders of magnitude:contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}.
  • In turbulence, highly deformable droplets (Ca ≈ 0.1) expedite energy dissipation; bubbles exert negligible influence on TKE spectra:contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}.
